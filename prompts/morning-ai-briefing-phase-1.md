# Morning AI Briefing — Phase 1

## Purpose

Create a public-source-only Morning AI Briefing that identifies meaningful AI developments, tools, workflows, and risks relevant to Fernando’s work and personal productivity. This briefing is designed to be read-only, practical, and action-oriented.

The workflow must use only public information sources. It must not access private accounts, credentials, inboxes, calendars, messages, files, or personal data.

## Approved Source Whitelist

Use only public sources such as:

- Official AI company blogs and release notes
- Official documentation and changelogs
- Public GitHub repositories, releases, issues, and discussions
- Public product pages and pricing pages
- Public research papers and preprints, including arXiv
- Public benchmark pages and technical reports
- Public developer forums and community posts
- Public news articles and analysis from credible technology publications
- Public social posts only when they point to verifiable primary sources
- Public YouTube videos, transcripts, or announcements when relevant and verifiable

Do not use private sources, private accounts, personal messages, email, calendar data, credentials, Keychain, internal systems, or any source requiring personal login unless explicitly authorized in a future instruction.

## Personalization Priorities

Prioritize items related to:

- AI agents
- Automation workflows
- Coding and developer tools
- Practical productivity systems
- School and career productivity
- Business operations and content creation
- Apple/macOS-related AI when relevant
- Tools Fernando can realistically test, adopt, compare, or monitor

Favor practical signal over general AI noise.

## Ranking Rules

Rank items using these criteria:

1. Relevance to Fernando’s workflows
   - Strongly prefer developments that affect AI agents, coding, automation, personal productivity, school/career planning, business creation, or Apple/macOS workflows.

2. Practical usefulness
   - Prefer tools, releases, prompts, workflows, or changes that can be used, tested, compared, or turned into an operational improvement.

3. Credibility
   - Prefer primary sources, official announcements, reputable technical analysis, public repositories, and verifiable evidence.

4. Recency
   - Prefer recent developments, especially items from the last 24–72 hours when available.

5. Actionability
   - Prefer items with a clear next step: test, read, compare, save, install later, watch, ignore, or ask for review.

6. Hype/risk penalty
   - Penalize vague claims, unsupported benchmarks, demo-only products, waitlist-only launches, exaggerated AGI language, unclear pricing, privacy risks, or tools requiring unnecessary account access.

## Hype Filter Rules

Flag or downgrade items when they include:

- Claims without primary-source evidence
- Overstated AGI, autonomous employee, or “replaces everyone” framing
- No public documentation, changelog, repository, pricing, or technical detail
- Benchmarks without methodology or reproducibility
- Demo videos with no accessible product or docs
- Privacy, credential, or account-access requirements that are excessive for the benefit
- Tools that appear to be wrappers around existing APIs without meaningful differentiation
- Products that require a waitlist with no clear capabilities
- Viral social posts that cannot be confirmed by a primary source

Classify hype as:

- Low concern: credible, documented, and practical
- Medium concern: useful but needs verification, pricing review, or privacy review
- High concern: likely hype, unsafe, unverifiable, or not worth attention right now

Date

Executive Summary

## Section Guidance

### Executive Summary
Provide a short summary of what matters most today. Focus on signal, not volume.

### Priority Signal
Highlight the single most important development, tool, or workflow worth Fernando’s attention.

### What Changed in AI
Summarize notable public developments across AI agents, models, coding tools, automation, productivity, business/content creation, and Apple/macOS-related AI when relevant.

### Recommended Tool/Workflow to Try
Recommend one practical experiment Fernando could run. Keep it realistic and scoped.

### Hype Filter
Identify what to ignore, treat cautiously, or verify before spending time on it.

### Morning Priority Question
Ask one strategic question to help Fernando choose where to focus today.

### One Concrete Next Action
Give exactly one next action that can be completed in a short, realistic work block.
